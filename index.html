What are bitcoins?
Bitcoin is a peer-to-peer payment system introduced as open source software in the year 2009 by a pseudo anonymous developer that goes by the name Satoshi Nakamoto. This system does not have any central regulators such as banks or financial institutions hence the term peer-to-peer. The question that you might be asking now is, why should I put my trust into this software technology if the creator is not even known? The system does not require any of your trust to actually work. Each transaction within the Bitcoin network is analyzed and confirmed by the people who make up the backbone, these individuals are called “miners”. Miners are individuals who use specialized pieces of hardware to perform immensely complicated calculations that enable the network to confirm transactions. The miners themselves are not able to modify the core behind Bitcoins, they just follow instructions from the specialized software that they run on their computers and are rewarded once a certain amount of transactions are confirmed.

Now that you have some backstory on the financial system that is Bitcoin, you might wonder why use Bitcoins? Bitcoin is not just the 181 currency in the world, it is the first decentralized virtual currency. Never before in our history have individuals been able to engage in transactions freely from anywhere in the world without the need of identification.
